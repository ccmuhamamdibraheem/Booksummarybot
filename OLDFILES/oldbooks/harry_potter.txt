Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone, written by J.K. Rowling, introduces readers to the magical world of wizards and the legendary Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The story begins with Harry Potter, an orphan raised by his cruel aunt and uncle, who discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is, in fact, a wizard. Whisked away into a world of spells, potions, and magical creatures, Harry forms deep friendships with Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger. Together, they begin their education at Hogwarts while uncovering secrets about the castle and its hidden chambers. Throughout the book, Harry learns more about his parents, who were murdered by the dark wizard Voldemort, and realizes that Voldemort is not truly gone but waiting for a chance to return. Themes include friendship, bravery, loyalty, the struggle between good and evil, and the discovery of one’s identity. The novel combines mystery, adventure, and coming-of-age struggles, marking the beginning of one of the most beloved and successful fantasy series in modern literature.
